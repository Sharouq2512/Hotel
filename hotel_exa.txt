Complete Hotel Management System Example
1. System Overview

The Hotel Management System is designed to manage daily hotel operations, improve guest services, and streamline administrative tasks. Core modules include:

Room Reservation and Booking

Guest Check-In/Check-Out

Housekeeping Management

Billing and Invoicing

Online Payments

Customer Feedback

Reporting and Analytics

User Account Management

The system is intended for guests, receptionists, housekeeping staff, accountants, and hotel managers.

2. User Stories (15 Stories)

Online Room Booking
As a guest, I want to book a room online so that I can secure my stay before arrival.
Acceptance Criteria: Guests can select dates, view available rooms, confirm booking, and receive email confirmation.
Story Points: 8 | Priority: High | Business Value: High

Check Room Availability
As a receptionist, I want to check real-time room availability so that I can manage reservations efficiently.
Acceptance Criteria: System shows all rooms with occupancy status and updates instantly.
Story Points: 5 | Priority: High | Business Value: High

Guest Check-In
As a receptionist, I want to check in guests quickly so that they can start their stay without delays.
Acceptance Criteria: Guest details verified, room assigned, key card issued, and room status updated.
Story Points: 5 | Priority: High | Business Value: High

Guest Check-Out
As a receptionist, I want to process guest check-out smoothly so that billing and room availability are updated.
Acceptance Criteria: Billing generated, payment confirmed, room status updated to available.
Story Points: 5 | Priority: High | Business Value: High

Housekeeping Schedule Management
As a housekeeping staff member, I want to view and update the daily cleaning schedule so that rooms are ready for guests.
Acceptance Criteria: Daily tasks displayed, status updated when completed, alerts for missed rooms.
Story Points: 3 | Priority: Medium | Business Value: Medium

Invoice Generation
As an accountant, I want to generate invoices for guests so that billing is accurate and consistent.
Acceptance Criteria: Invoices include room charges, taxes, additional services, and payment confirmation.
Story Points: 3 | Priority: High | Business Value: High

Online Payment
As a guest, I want to pay online for my reservation so that I can avoid paying in cash at check-in.
Acceptance Criteria: Secure payment gateway, payment confirmation, receipt sent via email.
Story Points: 8 | Priority: High | Business Value: High

Customer Feedback Submission
As a hotel manager, I want guests to submit feedback so that I can improve hotel services.
Acceptance Criteria: Guests can submit ratings/comments; system generates summary reports.
Story Points: 3 | Priority: Medium | Business Value: Medium

Report Generation
As a manager, I want to generate daily occupancy and revenue reports so that I can monitor hotel performance.
Acceptance Criteria: Reports display accurate occupancy, revenue, and guest statistics; can export PDF/Excel.
Story Points: 5 | Priority: Medium | Business Value: High

User Account Management
As a guest, I want to create and manage my account so that I can track reservations and payment history.
Acceptance Criteria: Guests can register, log in, update profile, and view past bookings.
Story Points: 5 | Priority: Medium | Business Value: Medium

Room Upgrade Option
As a guest, I want to request a room upgrade so that I can enjoy better facilities.
Acceptance Criteria: Guests can request upgrades; system confirms availability and cost.
Story Points: 3 | Priority: Low | Business Value: Medium

Promotions and Discounts
As a marketing manager, I want to apply promotions and discounts so that occupancy and revenue increase.
Acceptance Criteria: Discount codes applied correctly; reports track promotion impact.
Story Points: 3 | Priority: Low | Business Value: Medium

Multi-language Support
As an international guest, I want to use the system in my preferred language so that I can navigate easily.
Acceptance Criteria: System supports multiple languages; language selection remembered per user.
Story Points: 5 | Priority: Low | Business Value: Medium

Notifications and Alerts
As a receptionist, I want to receive alerts for upcoming reservations and check-outs so that I can prepare rooms in advance.
Acceptance Criteria: Alerts sent automatically for reservations/check-outs; configurable by user.
Story Points: 3 | Priority: Medium | Business Value: Medium

Loyalty Program Integration
As a guest, I want to earn loyalty points for stays so that I can redeem them for future discounts.
Acceptance Criteria: Points calculated for each stay, redemption tracked, loyalty balance displayed.
Story Points: 5 | Priority: Low | Business Value: Medium

3. Product Backlog with Weighting

The product backlog organizes these stories by story points, priority, and business value for sprint planning:

High Priority / High Business Value: Online Room Booking, Check Room Availability, Guest Check-In/Check-Out, Online Payment, Invoice Generation

Medium Priority / Medium Business Value: Housekeeping Management, Customer Feedback, Reporting, User Account Management, Notifications/Alerts

Low Priority / Medium Business Value: Room Upgrade, Promotions & Discounts, Multi-language Support, Loyalty Program

Story points guide effort estimation for sprint planning. High-point stories like Online Booking and Online Payment may span multiple sprints, while lower-point stories like Feedback or Alerts can be completed quickly.

4. Summary

This complete example demonstrates the application of SDLC concepts to a Hotel Management System, including:

User Stories written in standard format with acceptance criteria

Product Backlog organized by complexity, priority, and business value

Weighting and Estimation using story points for sprint planning

Coverage of Core Features such as booking, check-in/out, payments, housekeeping, reporting, and customer engagement
